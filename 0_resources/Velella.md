
### **What is this document?**

This is an overview of the Velella app and our process for implementing it. 

### **What is the Velella App?**

Velella is an app for planning a person's CoastFire future. Unlike simple "CoastFIRE Calculators," the user experience of Velella is centered around "scenario planning" where users build out a scenario in the form of a timeline of their future. They can hang narrative elements on the timeline ("have a baby," "Move back home", "Taking care of aging parents", etc...) to build out scenarios, then in assumptions about their income, savings, decumulation strategies, etc... 



#### **Who are the users?**

Velella is intended to be an app that multiple users can gather around and use together for scenario planning conversations. These may consist of a financial planner with her clients, or a couple planning their future together. In these situations, we can imagine that the collaborators will have different levels of financial sophistication while using the app. We can imagine, for instance, a couple with one partner who is more math-oriented and financially wonky than the other partner. That is why this app focuses life narrative: the timeline-oriented "scenario" format is a tool to build these different ways of thinking about planning. 

These users will likely be familiar with ideas like "Safe Withdrawl Rate" and the way that inflation and market returns play into their long term planning. These users are interested in "CoastFIRE", so it is unlikely that they will be thinking in "normal" terms of retirement planning. Rather, they'll be thinking about their lives in phases.



### **Our Build Process**

We will not build this app all in one large swoop. Instead, we are going to build it slowly, one feature at a time so we can iterate toward making it better. 

- **[Design Philosophy](/0_resources/design_philosophy.md)** outlines the core philosophy for our approach to code. Read it before making implementation plans. 
- **[Tickets](/0_resources/Tickets/)** contains the individual iterations of features that we're implementing. We do things one feature at a time, not as one huge code-base change. 
- **[Architecture and Dependencies](/0_resources/Architecture_and_Dependencies.md)** outlines the overall structure of the app. Read it to get an understanding of what is build so far, and ask the user if they want to update it whenever we make large changes. 
- **[PRD](/0_resources/PRD/)** is the Product Requirements Documents. These tell a UX-oriented story about how the app should work. It evolves over time as we build the app.
