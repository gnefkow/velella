## Velella is a CoastFIRE Scenario Planner.

---

Velella is an app built around a simple premise: there is no such thing as a "CoastFIRE Number." 

The notion of a "CoastFIRE Number" comes from the idea of a "FIRE" or "Financial Independence, Early Retirement)" number. Users pick their "Save Withdrawal Rate" (usually 4%, more info on that below), and use an equation to determine how large their portfolio needs to be based on their expenses. If you want to "retire" and spend $80,000 a year, you need a $2,000,000 investment portfolio. Simple!

> There is no such thing as a CoastFIRE Number.

CoastFIRE is much different. With CoastFIRE, we don't simply pick a "retirement date" and work hard to achieve it. Instead, we think of life in phases: some phases focused on "accumulation" (work hard, make a lot, invest a lot), some focused on "decumulation" (don't work at all, just spend like a normal "retirement") and "coast" years. In Coast years, you make just what you need: no excess for investing, but not pulling on your investment portfolio either. Make $80,000, spend $80,000. 

That is the simple theory, but we know that reality is a blend. Maybe for a few years you make $120,000 and spend $80k, investing $40k. Maybe you decide to be nomads for a while and stop making income at all but reducing your spending down to $30,000. Then you decide to work part time to raise your kids, so you're only making $70,000 and spending $70,000. 

If your life progresses in phases, how can you calculate your "CoastFIRE number?"

You can't. 

There is no such thing as a "CoastFIRE Number." 

However, SWR is still important: it is a heuristic that we can use to determine the health of our plan. This is where the Velella app comes in. 

## Plan life in "Eras"

Velella assumes that you didn't get into CoastFIRE so you could walk a linear path toward retirement. Instead, you're thinking about "eras" of life that each look different. As we think through these different eras, we want to know: does this take me off-track for retirement?

> I'd like to take a few years off to travel, does this take me off track for retirement?

> I'd like to cut down to part time work when my kids are young, does this take me off track to retirement?

> I'd like to quit my job and go live in Thailand working as a dive instructor for the next 10 years, does this take me off track to retirement? 

Velella takes a scenario-first approach to planning: sketch out a *scenario* of your life with its different *eras*, and see if the math adds up. If it does—go live your dreams. If it doesn't, see what you might be able to adjust. 



### Our goal is for Velella to be a conversation tool

The FIRE community is full of calculating math wonks (one of them built Velella). Our life partners' eyes tend to glaze over when we try to talk about life in terms of a spreadsheet. 

Our goal is that math wonks and normal people can sit together and discuss life plans. Feel free to play with the tool to go back and forth between the numbers and the plan. 

Don't expect to have all of the answers in an afternoon. Velella is a conversation tool: it will probably seed more questions than it answers at first. Contemplating hard questions is part of planning!
